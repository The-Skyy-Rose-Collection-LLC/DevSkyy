## Black Rose

*Collection 02 — The Refusal*

### Origin

Black Rose was born from Oakland's specific gravity — not the Bay, Oakland. Deep East, where beauty doesn't ask permission; it forces through the cracks. Corey named it for a posture before he named it for a flower. The collection launched in 2021 as part of the three-chapter debut alongside Signature and Love Hurts.

The founding premise, verbatim from `collection-content.php`:

> "A black rose is a posture. Not a flower — a conviction. In Oakland, where concrete is the only soil that matters, beauty doesn't ask for permission. It forces its way through the cracks."

Press recognition came external — Maxim (2023), San Francisco Post (2024), Best Bay Area Clothing Line Award (2024) — but Black Rose's identity predates and supersedes all of it. The collection is armor. The recognition is acknowledgment, not origin.

*Sources: `inc/collection-content.php`, `template-landing-black-rose.php`, `skyyrose.co/about/`*

### Voice & Mood

Declarative. No qualifiers. No softening. The voice says what is and moves on — Oakland taught that register. Not aggressive, not performative, just resolved.

Hero tagline (verbatim, live site): **"The beauty of the color black through the rose and high-end fashion design."**

Founder quote in `collection-content.php`:

> "Where I'm from, black isn't a color — it's armor. Every kid on my block knew that. Black Rose is that truth made into fabric. You don't wear it to stand out. You wear it because you already stood up."

Founder pull-quote from `template-parts/collection/founder-pullquote.php` (marked "Verbatim from brand-DNA"):

> "If you asked me four years ago, I never would have thought I'd be here. I had no drive, lost it all, a baby on the way, and was broke. But we knew we had to get it by any means necessary."

Mood board peers from `knowledge-base/seed/from-interview.md`: KITH, Palm Angels, FOG Eternal. Anti-references: no blue, no clichés, no dry reveals.

### Visual Language

- **Primary accent:** Silver `#C0C0C0` on black `#0A0A0A` background
- **Typography:** Cinzel for display headings (gothic restraint, classical weight); Hanken Grotesk for body
- **Motifs:** Thorn motif, dark-on-dark texture, monochrome mastery — no color pop, no contrast as decoration
- **Format:** Cinematic editorial. Not a grid. Every shot is a story beat.
- **Gestalt application:** Figure-ground contrast at maximum (silver on black ≥ 3 visual properties distinct); Continuity drives the eye along thorns toward the rose

*Design tokens from `assets/css/design-tokens.css`: `[data-collection="black-rose"]` block.*

### Narrative Arc

**Setup:** Oakland. The weight of a city that doesn't apologize. A father building something in the only soil available — concrete.

**Conflict:** The world says luxury doesn't come from here. The Town says otherwise.

**Resolution:** Every piece in Black Rose is the concrete answering back. The collection doesn't argue; it exists as proof.

Story text from `collection-content.php`:

> "They said luxury can't come from the Town. We said watch. Every piece in Black Rose is the concrete answering back."

The emotional journey moves from recognition (you know this weight) → refusal (you chose not to carry it on your face) → armor (you wear what you survived). Story completion lands when the customer understands they were always the protagonist.

### Story Tagline

**"You wear it because you already stood up."**

*(Lifted verbatim from founder quote in `collection-content.php`. Not invented.)*

## Love Hurts

*Collection 03 — The Grief*

### Origin

"Hurts" is not a theme. It is the bloodline that raised the founder.

Corey's own framing (locked 2026-05-23):

> "Hurts is the bloodline that raised me."

That is the line. Not maternal grandmother's surname, not extended family side — the people who raised him. The grandmother in `collection-content.php` is one voice from that bloodline; the "three generations of Hurts" on the landing page are the same people across time. Foster is the surname Corey carries publicly; Hurts is the line that shaped who carries it. Both are true. This collection is for the second one.

The narrative centers on a family that loved without stopping despite the world pouring pain back in return. The Beast & the rose motif runs through the collection — the Beast didn't hide from ugliness, he hid because he loved something fragile.

*Sources: `inc/collection-content.php`, `template-landing-love-hurts.php`, `skyyrose.co/about/`*

### Voice & Mood

Heavy but not defeated. The voice of someone who watched their family survive — not just endure, survive — and chose to name a collection after the survival instead of the wound.

Hero tagline (verbatim, live site): **"They called me Beast. They were right."**

Hero subtitle, verbatim: *"But even the Beast kept a rose under glass — protecting the most fragile thing he ever loved. This collection carries the weight of three generations of Hurts."*

Landing page story title, verbatim: *"This Isn't a Theme. It's a Family Name."*

Grandmother's voice, verbatim from `collection-content.php`:

> "My grandmother used to say it plain: 'Love hurts, baby. That's how you know it's real.' She wasn't being poetic. She was teaching survival."

Mood: *Beauty & the Beast cadence — luxury or witchcraft, take your pick.* (`skyyrose.co/about/` — About page description of Love Hurts)

### Visual Language

- **Primary accent:** Crimson `#DC143C`; palette extends to deep purple (bruises that became wisdom) and burgundy
- **Verbatim palette rationale from `collection-content.php`:** "Crimson for the blood we share. Deep purple for the bruises that became wisdom. Burgundy for the wine grandmama poured when she said, 'Baby, you survived another one.'"
- **Typography:** Archivo for headings; Hanken Grotesk for body
- **Motifs:** Heart-and-thorns, enchanted rose under glass, red rose clusters — visible on lh-004 Bomber: "Love" embroidered left chest, "Hurts" right chest, split by button-front placket; back features heart-and-thorns + red roses cluster; hood lining has sublimated red rose silhouettes on white
- **Format:** Emotional weight carried through silhouette and color density, not surface decoration
- **Gestalt:** Similarity links crimson accents across every garment as a visual category without a label; Closure drives the Beast/rose motif — the story the viewer completes in their head is the brand message

*Design tokens: `[data-collection="love-hurts"]` in `assets/css/design-tokens.css`.*

### Narrative Arc

**Setup:** A grandmother teaching survival through plain speech. Three generations of loving when love didn't love them back.

**Conflict:** The Beast's equation — something so beautiful it terrifies. The tension between protecting what you love and the world trying to take it.

**Resolution:** The rose under glass. Every member of the Hurts family who kept loving anyway is represented. The collection is the glass case.

Story quote, verbatim from `collection-content.php`:

> "The Beast didn't hide because he was ugly. He hid because he loved something so beautiful it terrified him. The rose under glass — that's every Hurts who ever kept loving when love didn't love them back."

Landing page parallax text: *"Wear Your Heart. Own Your Scars."*

Features: Beast's Vulnerability, The Enchanted Rose, The Transformation — three acts of the same story.

### Story Tagline

**"They called me Beast. They were right."**

*(Verbatim hero tagline, `collection-content.php`. Not invented.)*

## Signature

*Collection 01 — The Origin*

### Origin

Signature is Chapter One. The collection that started everything — drawn at 4 AM, broke, a baby on the way, every manufacturer having scammed the founder. The script logo sketched until it was right. This is the origin as Corey names it, not as outside observers frame it.

The founding moment, verbatim from `collection-content.php`:

> "I drew the first rose on a night I couldn't afford dinner. Broke, a baby on the way, every manufacturer I'd worked with had scammed me. But I sat there sketching that script logo until 4 AM because something in me knew — if I could get this right, everything changes. Signature is that night made permanent."

Landing page subtitle, verbatim: *"Not basics. Blueprints."*

About page description: *"The first rose, the first script. Where SkyyRose began — gold-accented luxury streetwear, gender-neutral by default."*

**Bay Bridge geography (locked 2026-05-23).** Corey's framing:

> "The Bay Bridge is Oakland. It connects Oakland to frisco."

The Bay Bridge SKUs (sg-001 Bay Bridge Shorts, sg-002/003 Stay Golden, sg-005 Bay Bridge Shirt) do not violate the Oakland-only brand canon — the bridge is an Oakland artifact. Frisco is what sits on the other end. Copy never frames Signature as "Bay Area"; the bridge is the Town's, not the bay's. "Frisco" is the locals' word for the city across the water and is voice-correct when SF is referenced at all.

*Sources: `inc/collection-content.php`, `template-landing-signature.php`, `skyyrose.co/about/`*

### Voice & Mood

Foundational. No nostalgia — this is not looking back, it's establishing bedrock. The tone of someone laying cornerstones. Quieter than Black Rose, more intimate than Love Hurts. The origin chapter has confidence without performance.

Landing page story: *"Signature is the collection that started everything. Before the limited drops and the press features, there was a father in Oakland who believed everyday clothes should feel like something."*

Founder quote from `collection-content.php`:

> "They told me luxury doesn't come from Oakland. I said luxury grows from concrete — and I meant that literally. This collection is the concrete. Everything else grew from here."

Verbatim founder quote also present in `template-parts/collection/founder-pullquote.php`:

> "Four years ago I never would have thought I'd be here. I had no drive, lost it all, a baby on the way, and was broke. But we knew we had to get it by any means necessary."

Mood peers (aligned 2026-05-25 to locked canon at `docs/brand/visual-references.md` — The Five): **Kith** (monogram-led editorial), **Aimé Leon Dore** (acceptable adjacency, retail-anchored editorial), **Fear of God** (cinematic spareness). The Row reference retired — European-luxury-house lineage is locked out for SkyyRose. Gender-neutral by default.

### Visual Language

- **Primary accent:** Gold `#D4AF37`
- **Typography:** Archivo for headings; Hanken Grotesk for body
- **Badge:** "Where It All Began" — used in hero block of `collection-content.php`
- **Label:** "Chapter One" / title "The First Rose" — the framing is literary, not product-catalog
- **Motifs:** Script logo as anchor; gold on dark; the rose before the thorns
- **Format:** Editorial but warmer than Black Rose. This is origin story, not armor.
- **Gestalt:** Continuity aligns the gold thread across disparate silhouettes, making the full collection read as one sentence; Proximity clusters the "everyday elevated" message (landing parallax: *"Everyday Doesn't Mean Ordinary"*)

*Design tokens: `:root` defaults in `assets/css/design-tokens.css` (Signature is the base collection; all others override from here).*

### Narrative Arc

**Setup:** Oakland, 2020. A man with nothing but the conviction that everyday clothes should feel like something.

**Conflict:** The night he couldn't afford dinner, sketching until 4 AM. Every manufacturer had scammed him. The world said this city doesn't produce luxury.

**Resolution:** The script logo gets right. Signature is that night made permanent. Everything the brand became grew from this collection as foundation.

Landing page parallax: *"Everyday Doesn't Mean Ordinary."*

The arc lands on the customer understanding they are wearing the proof — not the promise, the proof.

### Story Tagline

**"Not basics. Blueprints."**

*(Verbatim landing page subtitle, `template-landing-signature.php`. Not invented.)*

## Kids Capsule

*Collection 04 — The Heir*

### Origin

The brand is named after her — Skyy Rose, Corey's daughter. She was on the way when he had nothing. No drive, no money, no support. She was the reason he built.

Verbatim from `collection-content.php`:

> "The whole brand is named after her — Skyy Rose. My daughter. She was on the way when I had nothing. No drive, no money, no support. But that baby coming changed everything. I needed to build something she'd be proud to carry."

The Kids Capsule is the brand completing its circle — the daughter who was the reason for all of it now wears the brand built in her name. Launched 2026 per the About page timeline: *"The fourth chapter. Same craftsmanship, smaller silhouettes. Passing the torch on the same terms that built the brand."*

Products: kids-001 Red/Black Hoodie Set ($65), kids-002 Purple/Black Hoodie Set ($65). Two SKUs. The capsule is tight by design — the same precision, smaller scale.

*Sources: `inc/collection-content.php`, `skyyrose.co/about/`, `skyyrose-catalog.csv`*

### Voice & Mood

Legacy. No sentimentality — this isn't nostalgia, it's inheritance. The same declarative voice as the rest of the brand, now aimed at the next generation.

Hero tagline (verbatim, live site): **"Luxury runs in the family."**

Hero subtitle, verbatim: *"Premium streetwear for the next generation — powerful, elevated, and born into legacy. Because legacy is not inherited. It is worn."*

Story label: "Her Name" / Story title: "Named After My Daughter"

Founder quote from `collection-content.php`:

> "I built SkyyRose so my daughter would never have to wonder if she was enough."

Founder's mandate for the product, verbatim:

> "No pastels. No cartoons. Skyy Rose doesn't wear that. She wears what her father built — premium, dark, elegant. Scaled down but never dumbed down."

Mood from `skyyrose.co/about/` description: *"Rose gold and soft pink. The fourth chapter, smaller silhouettes, same craftsmanship."*

### Visual Language

- **Primary accent:** Rose Gold `#B76E79` (global brand accent; Kids Capsule is its canonical home)
- **Typography:** Archivo for headings; Hanken Grotesk for body
- **Palette:** Rose gold + soft pink, but NO pastels, NO cartoons — the softness is in the color, not the attitude
- **Silhouette:** Scaled down, never dumbed down. Same construction weight, smaller form.
- **Current products:** Red/Black and Purple/Black colorways — dark-first, matching the brand's primary register even in kids wear
- **Format:** The editorial frame treats the child as the protagonist, not as a cute accessory to adult brand messaging
- **Gestalt:** Similarity links Rose Gold across KC pieces to the global brand token; the collection reads as family resemblance, not separate line

*Design tokens: `[data-collection="kids-capsule"]` in `assets/css/design-tokens.css` (uses global Rose Gold `#B76E79` as default `--skyyrose-accent`).*

### Narrative Arc

**Setup:** A father in Oakland who needed a reason. A daughter whose name became the brand.

**Conflict:** The gap between what he had (nothing) and what she deserved (everything). Not resolved by denial — resolved by building.

**Resolution:** She wears what he built. Same craftsmanship, her scale. The brand completes its circle without sentimentality.

Verbatim story quote:

> "I built SkyyRose so my daughter would never have to wonder if she was enough."

The emotional journey is the simplest and heaviest in the brand's portfolio: reason → absence → proof. Customers who are parents feel it immediately. Customers who know what it means to be built for — rather than marketed to — feel it too.

### Story Tagline

**"Luxury runs in the family."**

*(Verbatim hero tagline, `collection-content.php`. Not invented.)*
