1. Scope & Who We Are

SkyyRose LLC ("SkyyRose," "we," "our," or "us") is a California limited liability company operating the luxury streetwear brand and online store at https://skyyrose.co ("the Site"). Our principal place of business is in Oakland, California.

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information collected through the Site, our WooCommerce-powered store, and any related communications or services we provide. It does not apply to third-party websites or services that may be linked from our Site.

For questions about this policy, contact us at privacy@skyyrose.co.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal information:

A. Identifiers

  • Name (first and last)
  • Email address
  • Shipping and billing address
  • Phone number (if provided)
  • IP address and device identifiers
  • Account username and password (hashed)

B. Commercial Information

  • Order history (products purchased, quantities, prices)
  • Cart contents and wish lists
  • Return and exchange records
  • Pre-order records and estimated ship dates

C. Internet / Device Activity

  • Pages visited, time on page, referring URLs
  • Browser type and version, operating system
  • Clickstream data, search queries on the Site
  • Cookie identifiers and session tokens (see Section 7 and our Cookie Policy)
  • Advertising interaction data (ad views, clicks) via TikTok pixel and Google Analytics advertising features

D. Approximate Geolocation

We infer a general geographic location (city/region/country) from your IP address for analytics, fraud prevention, and currency/shipping-rate calculation. We do not collect precise GPS-level location data.

E. Payment Information

Payment card numbers and financial account details are collected and processed directly by our payment processors (Stripe and PayPal). We do not store payment card data on our servers. We retain only non-sensitive confirmation details such as the last four digits of a card, card type, and transaction identifiers returned by the processor.

8. Your California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018 as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020 (CCPA/CPRA) grants you the following rights with respect to your personal information:

Right to Know

You may request that we disclose: the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you; the categories of sources from which we collected it; the business or commercial purpose for collecting or sharing it; and the categories of third parties to whom we disclosed it.

Right to Delete

You may request deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions (such as completing a transaction, detecting security incidents, or complying with a legal obligation).

Right to Correct

You may request correction of inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.

Right to Opt Out of Sale / Sharing

You have the right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. See Section 9 for how to exercise this right.

Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information

To the extent we process "sensitive personal information" as defined by CPRA (such as precise geolocation or financial account details), you may have the right to limit our use of such information to purposes necessary to provide the services you requested. We do not use sensitive personal information for any purpose beyond service delivery, and payment card data is handled exclusively by our PCI-compliant processors.

Right to Non-Discrimination

We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA/CPRA rights. We will not deny you goods or services, charge you a different price, or provide you a different level of quality because you exercised a privacy right.

9. Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information

Although we do not sell personal information for money, our use of the TikTok pixel and Google Analytics advertising features (including remarketing and audience-building) constitutes "sharing" of personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising under the CPRA. You have the right to opt out of this sharing.

How to opt out

  • Email request: Send an email to privacy@skyyrose.co with the subject line "Do Not Share My Personal Information." We will process your request within 15 business days.
  • Cookie controls: You may disable advertising and analytics cookies through our Cookie Policy consent tool or through your browser settings. Disabling these cookies will prevent the TikTok pixel and Google advertising cookies from operating on your device.
  • Global Privacy Control (GPC): We honor the Global Privacy Control browser signal. If your browser or browser extension transmits a GPC signal, we will treat it as an opt-out of the sharing of your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising from that browser on that device. Note that the GPC signal is device- and browser-specific and does not automatically apply to your account or other devices.
  • Platform-level opt-outs: You may also opt out of interest-based advertising through the TikTok app settings and through Google's Ad Settings.

Opting out will not affect your ability to place orders or use any Site features.

10. Your Rights Under GDPR / UK GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, you have the following rights regarding your personal data under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and, where applicable, the UK GDPR:

  • Right of access (Art. 15): Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16): Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17): Request deletion of your personal data, subject to certain exceptions (legal obligations, contractual necessity).
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18): Request that we limit the way we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20): Receive personal data you have provided to us in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, where technically feasible.
  • Right to object (Art. 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@skyyrose.co. We will respond within 30 days (extendable by a further two months for complex requests, with notice to you).

Right to lodge a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority. For EEA residents, the relevant authority is in your member state of habitual residence, place of work, or the place of the alleged infringement. For UK residents, the relevant authority is the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

International data transfers

SkyyRose LLC is based in the United States. When we transfer personal data from the EEA or UK to the United States or other countries that may not provide the same level of data protection as your home country, we rely on appropriate safeguards, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission (and, for the UK, the UK Addendum to SCCs), or other lawful transfer mechanisms. You may request a copy of the applicable transfer mechanism by contacting privacy@skyyrose.co.

EU / UK Representative

SkyyRose LLC has determined that it is not required to appoint an EU or UK representative under Article 27 of the GDPR / UK GDPR. EEA and UK residents may exercise their rights or raise concerns by contacting us directly at privacy@skyyrose.co.

12. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy or as required by applicable law, whichever is longer. Key retention considerations include:

  • Order and transaction records are retained for a minimum of seven (7) years for tax and accounting compliance.
  • Account information is retained for the duration of your account; if you close your account, we will delete or anonymize your data within a reasonable period, subject to legal obligations.
  • Marketing preferences and communications are retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
  • Analytics and log data is typically retained for up to 26 months (Google Analytics default) or as configured; IP-level data is anonymized sooner.

When personal information is no longer needed and no legal obligation requires its retention, we delete it or anonymize it so it can no longer be linked to you.

13. Data Security

We implement reasonable and appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information against unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. Specific measures include:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data in transit between your browser and the Site (HTTPS).
  • PCI-DSS compliance for payment card data, handled exclusively by Stripe and PayPal — card numbers are never transmitted through or stored on our servers.
  • Access controls limiting access to personal data to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis.

No method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach that affects your rights and freedoms, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by applicable law.
