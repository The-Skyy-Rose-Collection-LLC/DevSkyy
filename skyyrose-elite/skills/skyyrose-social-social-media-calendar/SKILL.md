# SkyyRose Social — Social Media Calendar

## When to Use This Skill

- Planning the upcoming week's or month's posts across all active SkyyRose platforms
- Assigning the correct collection voice to each scheduled content slot
- Filling in a monthly calendar that accounts for drop windows and Monthly Specials
- Generating a batch-creation guide so one shoot session covers a full week
- Onboarding a social coordinator who needs a repeatable daily structure

**DO NOT** use this skill for building the overall platform strategy (use `skyyrose-social-social-media-strategy`) or writing individual post captions (use `skyyrose-social-instagram-carousel`, `skyyrose-social-tiktok-script`). This skill handles scheduling and structure, not content writing.

---

## Brand Canon (non-negotiable)

- **Tagline is verbatim:** `Luxury Grows from Concrete.` Period included. Use in Friday exclusivity posts and Month 4 drop hype — never paraphrase.
- **Daily collection voice discipline:** Every planned slot must note which collection it represents — and that collection's voice is locked. Black Rose = armor. Love Hurts = bloodline. Signature = the standard. Kids = little royalty.
- **No urgency-manipulation language** in any calendar slot description. Exclusivity is stated as fact, not manufactured pressure.
- **Products by NAME in every slot description.** "BLACK Rose Crewneck" not "br-001". Downstream creators pull from calendar descriptions.
- **Oakland-first framing** in Brand Story and Culture slots. Bay Area acceptable; The Town preferred.

Full canon: ../skyyrose-content-engine/brand-guardrails.md

## Phase 3: Write

### Daily Stories (in addition to feed posts)

- 2-3 Stories per day minimum

## Anti-Patterns

- **No collection voice notation in slots** — If a calendar slot doesn't specify the collection register, whoever writes the caption may default to wrong language. Every slot must name the collection.
- **Identical posting times on all platforms** — IG Reels and TikTok share similar audience but different prime-time windows. TikTok peaks later in the evening; schedule accordingly.
- **Over-planning 4+ weeks ahead** — Week 3+ content should be blocked themes only, not specific topics. Drop windows shift; locked-in topics become stale.
- **No flexibility buffer** — Leave at least one slot per week unassigned for reactive cultural moments or trending audio opportunities.
- **Monthly Specials ignored** — Community Week (Week 3) consistently gets skipped in favor of product content. Community content drives the trust that makes product drops convert.
- **Sunday always skipped** — Engagement-only Sundays feel low-value but they drive the algorithmic favor that amplifies Monday's Brand Story post. Do not skip.

---

## Recovery

- **Missed posting days:** Do not batch-post 3x in one day to "catch up". Skip the missed slot and resume the next scheduled post. Cramming signals desperation, not consistency.
- **No UGC for Wednesday:** Run a Community slot with an Oakland cultural moment (a piece of Bay Area history, a local event, a cultural reference that fits the brand) instead. Never manufacture UGC.
- **Shoot day cancelled:** Reschedule the week's video slots to the following week and fill with existing asset repurposes — an older carousel slide as a static post, a Pinterest pin cross-posted to IG Stories.
- **Calendar not performing:** Change one variable at a time — first test post time, then format, then caption hook. Never overhaul the entire calendar because one week underperformed.
- **Cannot batch-create content:** Block 90 minutes every Sunday to create the next week's text posts. Protect that window. A small consistent calendar beats an ambitious abandoned one.
