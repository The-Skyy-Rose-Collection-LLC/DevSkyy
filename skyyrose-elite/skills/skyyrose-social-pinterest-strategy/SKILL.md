# SkyyRose Social — Pinterest Strategy

## When to Use This Skill

- Setting up or rebuilding SkyyRose's Pinterest presence from scratch
- Designing board structures that map to the 4 SkyyRose collections
- Creating keyword-rich pin descriptions that surface in fashion + streetwear searches
- Optimizing WooCommerce product pins to link directly to skyyrose.co product pages
- Building a scheduling cadence for long-term traffic compound growth
- Generating a 90-day plan after a Pinterest audit identifies gaps

**DO NOT** use this skill for other social platforms — Pinterest's SEO-search engine mechanic is fundamentally different from Instagram or TikTok. Do not apply Instagram engagement logic here. Pinterest is long-tail search, not social engagement.

---

## Brand Canon (non-negotiable)

- **Tagline verbatim:** `Luxury Grows from Concrete.` Use in profile bio and in board descriptions. Never paraphrase.
- **Oakland-first keyword anchor.** "Oakland fashion", "Oakland streetwear", "Bay Area style" are primary SEO targets on Pinterest — they differentiate SkyyRose from generic luxury streetwear.
- **The Five visual references.** Pin aesthetics reference Kith (editorial polish), Culture Kings (bold graphic streetwear), Fear of God (quiet luxury silhouettes), Oaklandish (local pride, community rootedness), Palm Angels (street-luxe confidence). No European luxury-house mood boards.
- **Collection voice in pin descriptions.** Even in keyword-dense descriptions, the collection's emotional register should be detectable: Black Rose = armor/defiant, Love Hurts = raw passion, Signature = elevated/golden, Kids = heritage/playful-premium.
- **Product pins link to WooCommerce product pages** — not to the homepage. Every product pin is a direct entry point to the purchase flow.

### 3. Pin Strategy

| Pin Type | Purpose | Frequency |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Standard product pin (Rich Pin)** | Drive traffic to WooCommerce product pages | 50% of pins |
| **Lifestyle pin** | Show product in Oakland/Bay Area context | 30% of pins |
| **Idea pin** | Build reach + followers (no external link) | 15% of pins |
| **Video pin** | High engagement — repurposed from TikTok/Reels | 5% of pins |

**Standard pin design (1000x1500px, 2:3 ratio):**

```
Template A — Product Hero
- Dark background (#0A0A0A)
- Product centered, full garment visible
- Collection accent color border or text accent (Silver/Crimson/Gold/Rose Gold)
- Bold 4-6 word headline: "Built for Oakland Winters"
- SkyyRose wordmark bottom center
- CTA text: "Shop at skyyrose.co"

Template B — Lifestyle Context
- Oakland/Bay Area location shot background (or studio with environment cues)
- Product worn — model in environment
- Minimal text overlay: product name only
- SkyyRose logo bottom-right

Template C — Detail Close-Up
- Fabric, embroidery, or hardware macro shot
- 2-3 words: "Crafted. Every detail."
- CTA: "skyyrose.co"
```

**Create 3-5 pin variations per product:**
- Different headline
- Different background treatment
- Different CTA text
- Same product, maximized Pinterest surface area through variation

### 4. Keyword Strategy (Pinterest SEO)

Pinterest functions as a visual search engine. Keywords placed correctly drive discovery.

**Where to use keywords:**
- Profile name and bio
- Board titles and descriptions (most impactful)
- Pin titles (max 100 characters — primary keyword in first 40 chars)
- Pin descriptions (first 50 characters are weighted most heavily by the algorithm)
- Image file names before upload: `black-rose-crewneck-oakland-streetwear.jpg` not `IMG_4521.jpg`

**SkyyRose primary keyword targets:**

| Target Keyword | Volume Signal | Content Type |
|----------------|--------------|-------------|
| `luxury streetwear` | High | Product + lifestyle |
| `Oakland fashion` | Mid | Lifestyle + culture |
| `Black-owned clothing brand` | Mid | Brand story + product |
| `Oakland streetwear` | Niche | Product + lifestyle |
| `Black designer fashion` | Niche | Product + brand story |
| `Bay Area style` | Niche | Lifestyle + culture |
| `men's luxury streetwear` | Mid | Styling + product |
| `women's streetwear style` | Mid | Styling + product |
| `Black Rose Collection streetwear` | Micro | Collection-specific product |
| `independent fashion brand` | Niche | Brand story + product |

**Find Pinterest-specific keywords:**
1. Pinterest search bar autocomplete — type "luxury streetwear" and note autocomplete suggestions
2. Keyword bubbles in search results — click first result and note the category tags that appear
3. Pinterest Trends tool — check seasonal peaks for "streetwear" and "Oakland fashion"

**Pin description formula:**

```
"[Benefit-driven sentence with primary keyword]. [Supporting detail with secondary keyword].
[Oakland/Bay Area anchor]. [CTA]. #luxurystreetwear #oaklandfashion"

Example (BLACK Rose Crewneck):
"Luxury streetwear built for the streets of Oakland. The BLACK Rose Crewneck is
crafted for those who already stood up — heavyweight construction, embroidered
chest detail, made to last. Black-owned fashion from The Town. Shop at skyyrose.co.
#luxurystreetwear #oaklandfashion #blackownedfashion"
```

### 5. Rich Pins Setup

Rich Pins pull real-time product data (price, availability, name) from WooCommerce, making product pins dramatically more valuable for purchase-intent users.

**Setup steps:**
1. Ensure `wordpress-theme/skyyrose-flagship/` has Yoast SEO installed and active
2. Enable "Open Graph" meta data in Yoast settings
3. Go to Pinterest → Settings → Claim → Add website → Verify skyyrose.co
4. Apply for Rich Pins at developers.pinterest.com/tools/url-debugger
5. Use any skyyrose.co product URL as the test URL
6. Once approved, all product pins from skyyrose.co auto-pull product name, price, availability

**Verification check:** After approval, product pins should show price and availability below the image. If not, check Yoast Open Graph tags on product pages.

### 6. Scheduling Cadence

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| New product pins (own content) | 5-8/week |
| Lifestyle pins (own content) | 3-5/week |
| Curated repins (relevant boards) | 5-10/week |
| Idea pins | 1-2/week |
| Video pins (repurposed from TikTok) | 1-2/week |
| **Total pins per week** | **15-25** |

**Scheduling tool:** Tailwind (recommended — Pinterest-native scheduler with SmartSchedule for optimal timing) or Pinterest native scheduler.

**Batch creation session (2 hours/week):**
```
Hour 1: Create 15 pin designs in Canva using the 3 templates
        → Template A: 5 product hero pins across collections
        → Template B: 5 lifestyle pins from existing IG/TikTok assets
        → Template C: 5 detail close-ups from Elite Studio golden images

Hour 2: Write keyword-rich descriptions for all 15 pins.
        Schedule in Tailwind across 7 days.
        Add 5-10 curated repins to fill the board with relevant content.
```

**Best times:** Pinterest performs well evenings (7-9pm) and weekends — Tailwind SmartSchedule handles this automatically based on your audience's activity.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Treating Pinterest like Instagram** — Pinterest is a search engine. Keyword placement in descriptions, file names, and board descriptions drives discovery. Hashtags and engagement matter less than on IG. Optimize for search.
- **Pinning once per product** — One pin per product means one chance at discovery. Create 3-5 pin variations per product with different headlines, images, and color treatments. More pins = more indexed surfaces.
- **Empty board descriptions** — "Black Rose Collection" as a board title with no description is invisible to Pinterest's search index. Every board needs a keyword-rich 2-3 sentence description.
- **Linking product pins to the homepage** — Pinterest users have purchase intent. A pin that lands on skyyrose.co's homepage loses that intent. Every product pin must deep-link to the specific WooCommerce product page.
- **Skipping Rich Pins** — Manual product pins require updating price/availability manually. Rich Pins pull real-time data from WooCommerce. Skip this setup and every pin is outdated the moment pricing or availability changes.
- **Only curating, never creating** — Curated repins grow boards but don't drive traffic to skyyrose.co. SkyyRose pins must be 60% of each board's content.
- **Inconsistent pin dimensions** — Pinterest's algorithm prefers 2:3 vertical (1000x1500px). Square pins get less real estate. Horizontal pins are deprioritized. All pins should be vertical.

## Recovery

- **Zero traffic after 60 days:** Audit in order: (1) Are product pins linking to correct WooCommerce pages? (2) Are pin descriptions keyword-rich with primary terms in the first 50 characters? (3) Are there at least 15-20 pins/week being published? Fix the lowest-numbered failure first.
- **No product images in correct format:** Pull from `skyyrose/elite_studio/assets/golden/[sku]/front.jpg` and crop to 2:3 in Canva. Elite Studio golden assets are the canonical source.
- **Rich Pins not activating:** Verify Yoast Open Graph is enabled on WooCommerce product post types. Then re-submit the URL at Pinterest's Rich Pin validator. Allow 24-48 hours for approval.
- **Cannot design pins:** Use Canva Pinterest templates. Customize with `#0A0A0A` background, collection accent color, and SkyyRose wordmark. No design skill required — the brand rules do the design work.
- **Account was flagged/penalized:** Review Pinterest's spam policies. The most common cause is bulk pinning the same link to multiple boards simultaneously. Spread pins across time and boards. Rebuild with compliant cadence.
