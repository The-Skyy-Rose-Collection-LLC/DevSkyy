# SkyyRose Social — YouTube Strategy

## When to Use This Skill

- Planning SkyyRose's YouTube channel from the ground up
- Defining content pillars and upload cadence that match production capacity
- Building thumbnail and title guidelines that maintain SkyyRose's visual identity
- Mapping the first 10 videos before the channel launches
- Creating a 6-month roadmap from zero to brand-authority channel

**DO NOT** use this skill for writing individual TikTok or Instagram Reels scripts (use `skyyrose-social-tiktok-script` and `skyyrose-social-short-form-video-plan`). YouTube is long-form brand authority. This is channel-level strategy, not individual video scripts.

---

## Brand Canon (non-negotiable)

- **Tagline verbatim:** `Luxury Grows from Concrete.` Use in channel description, About page, and end-card of every video.
- **Oakland-first:** Channel identity is rooted in The Town. Bay Area acceptable; Oakland is the anchor. Never "West Coast streetwear" as the primary descriptor when "Oakland" is available and accurate.
- **The Five visual references only:** Thumbnails, B-roll mood, and production aesthetic draw from Kith · Oaklandish · Culture Kings · Fear of God · Palm Angels. No European luxury-house editorial framing.
- **Corey is the protagonist, not just a face.** YouTube rewards founder-voice authenticity. Corey's earned, direct, Oakland register is the channel's competitive advantage. Do not produce a "brand channel" — produce Corey's channel, with SkyyRose as the subject.
- **No hype-merchant energy.** No "SUBSCRIBE NOW!!! 🔥🔥" end-card copy. CTA language is direct and specific: "Watch the collection walkthrough next" or "Pre-order link in description."

### 3. Upload Schedule

| Format | Frequency | Best Publish Day | Best Time (PST) |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Long-form video | 1-2x/month minimum | Wednesday or Thursday | 9:00 AM |
| YouTube Shorts | 3-5x/week | Daily, Tuesday–Saturday | 9:00 AM or 12:00 PM |

**Consistency rule:** 1 long-form/month + 3 Shorts/week beats 4 long-form uploads in one month and silence for three. Shorts drive subscriber growth faster and compound long-form discovery.

### 4. Title & Thumbnail System

**Title formulas (SkyyRose-adapted):**

```
Formula 1: "Inside [Collection Name] — [What They'll See]"
  → "Inside the Black Rose Collection — Every Piece, Explained"

Formula 2: "I Built [Thing] in Oakland. Here's Why."
  → "I Built a Luxury Streetwear Brand from Oakland. Here's Why."

Formula 3: "Why [Common Thing] Is Wrong (And What I Do Instead)"
  → "Why 'Luxury Streetwear' Usually Misses the Point"

Formula 4: "[Number] Things Nobody Tells You About [Topic]"
  → "5 Things Nobody Tells You About Running an Independent Fashion Brand"

Formula 5: "The [Product Name] — What It Is, Why It Exists"
  → "The BLACK Rose Crewneck — What It Is, Why It Exists"
```

**Title rules:**
- Under 60 characters for full visibility without truncation
- Front-load the most compelling word or name (collection name, "Black Rose", "Oakland")
- Never start with "SkyyRose" — the channel already signals the brand. Start with the hook.
- No ALL-CAPS click-bait. No "YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS". Corey's voice is earned, not hyped.

**Thumbnail system:**

```
## SkyyRose Thumbnail Template

Base: Dark background (#0A0A0A or dark editorial image)
Accent: Collection accent color (Silver for Black Rose, Crimson for Love Hurts,
        Gold for Signature, Rose Gold for brand-wide)
Text: 3-5 bold words — NOT the video title. Complementary hook.
      Example: Title = "Inside the Black Rose Collection"
               Thumbnail text = "BUILT FOR THE TOWN"
Face: Corey if on-camera content. Product if no face available.
      Expression should convey directness — not forced excitement.
Style reference: Fear of God tonal restraint + Culture Kings bold graphic confidence
Logo: SkyyRose wordmark bottom-right corner, small

Dimensions: 1280x720px (16:9)
Test: Squint test — is the thumbnail legible at phone-thumbnail size? If not, simplify.
```

### 5. Video Structure

**Standard format (applies to all Pillars 1-4):**

```
[0:00-0:30] HOOK
- State what the viewer will get in the first 15 seconds
- "In this video I'm walking you through every piece of the Black Rose Collection —
   what it is, why I made it, and how to get it before the pre-order closes."
- No long intros. No "Hey everyone, welcome back to my channel."
  Corey's register is direct. Start with the point.

[0:30-8:00] BODY
- Follow the structure promised in the hook
- Pattern interrupt every 2-3 minutes: cut to product B-roll, location shot,
  production footage, or still image with VO
- Chapters / timestamps for videos over 8 minutes — always add them
- Keep the Corey voice: earned, specific, Oakland-direct. No performance.

[8:00-end] CTA (final 60 seconds)
- Single, specific CTA: "Link to pre-order is in the description —
  limited run, once they're gone, they're gone."
- Suggest next video: "If you want to see how this piece gets made,
  watch the production video next" + point to end card
- End card: Subscribe button + suggested next video
- Final line: "Luxury Grows from Concrete." — let it land.
```

**Target lengths by pillar:**
- Collection Deep-Dives: 10-15 minutes
- Brand Story / Founder: 8-12 minutes
- Behind the Production: 6-10 minutes
- Oakland / Culture: 6-10 minutes
- Shorts: Under 60 seconds (from existing TikTok/Reels)

### 6. Shorts Strategy

SkyyRose already produces TikTok and Reels content. Every TikTok is a potential Short:

```
Repurposing pipeline:
  TikTok (native 9:16) → Upload to YouTube Shorts as-is OR re-edit with YT-specific CTA

Shorts content types:
  - Product reveal clips (15-30s) from existing TikToks
  - "One thing about [product]" clips — 30-45s insight with product close-up
  - Collection teaser cuts from long-form videos — drive viewers to full video

Shorts frequency: 3-5x/week from existing assets (no new shoots required)
Shorts CTA: "Full collection walkthrough on the channel" or "Pre-order link in bio"
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Generic "streetwear brand" positioning** — "Oakland luxury streetwear built by the founder" is differentiating. "Premium streetwear for those who want more" is every brand. Be specific.
- **Long intros** — "Hey guys, welcome back, make sure you hit subscribe before you forget..." — Corey's register is direct. Start with the point within the first 15 seconds.
- **Producing long-form before the Shorts engine is running** — Shorts from the existing TikTok backlog launch the channel and build subscriber base before long-form demands bandwidth. Don't skip Shorts.
- **Thumbnails without the dark background** — SkyyRose's visual identity is `#0A0A0A` dark. A light, bright thumbnail looks like a different brand. Consistency beats click-bait novelty.
- **Multiple CTAs in one video** — "Subscribe, comment, like, share, buy, visit the website, follow on IG..." Pick one. Every video ends with one specific next action tied to the video's content.
- **Chasing YouTube trends that don't fit** — Viral YouTube formats (reaction videos, mukbangs, challenge formats) aren't SkyyRose. Adapt the platform to the brand's voice, not the reverse.

---

## Recovery

- **No views on first 5 videos:** Audit thumbnails and titles first — click-through rate (CTR) below 4% means the packaging is the problem, not the content. Redesign thumbnails before producing more videos.
- **Can't commit to monthly uploads:** Switch to Shorts-only strategy. Upload 3-5 Shorts/week from the TikTok backlog. No new production required. Build subscriber base before adding long-form demands.
- **Running out of video topics:** Every collection has 4+ angles: walkthrough, production, founder story, styling. 4 collections = 16+ long-form topics before you need to expand beyond the catalog.
- **Corey unavailable for on-camera content:** Produce Pillar 3 (Behind the Production) and Pillar 4 (Oakland Culture) as voice-over over B-roll — no face required. Quality VO over strong visuals maintains brand authority.
- **Subscriber growth stalling after Month 3:** The Shorts pipeline is the fastest lever. If Shorts aren't running at 3x/week, start there. Every Short drives algorithm recommendation to new potential subscribers.
