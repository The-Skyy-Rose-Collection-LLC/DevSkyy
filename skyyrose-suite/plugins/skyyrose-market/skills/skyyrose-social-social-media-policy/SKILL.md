# SkyyRose Social — Social Media Policy

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing or revising the formal SkyyRose Social Media Policy for brand accounts and any team representative
- Defining who is authorized to post from official SkyyRose accounts (Instagram, TikTok, X, Pinterest)
- Documenting brand voice standards and platform-specific tone rules for any collaborator or community champion
- Creating the content approval workflow for announcements, drops, and sensitive topics
- Building the crisis escalation protocol for social media incidents
- Documenting FTC and legal compliance requirements for sponsored content and partnerships

**DO NOT** use this for creating social content (use `skyyrose-content-engine`), for community moderation rules (use `skyyrose-social-community-moderation`), or for the public-facing community guidelines (use `skyyrose-social-platform-community-guidelines`). This is a governance document for internal and team use.

---

## Brand Canon (non-negotiable)

- **Tagline (exact):** `Luxury Grows from Concrete.` — this is the only tagline. Anyone posting on behalf of SkyyRose must use it verbatim when referencing the brand positioning. Never "grown from concrete," never "luxury from the streets."
- **Voice guardrails:** Oakland-direct, earned, unhurried. The brand does not hype. Any post that reads like a hype-merchant wrote it violates this policy.
- **No urgency manipulation.** Scarcity statements must be factual ("limited to pre-order run of 250") — never fake countdown pressure. Anyone posting urgency timers violates this policy.
- **Products by name, not SKU.** Any team member or partner who references a SkyyRose product in a post must use the product name (e.g., "BLACK Rose Crewneck"), not a SKU code.
- **Collection voice boundaries.** Team members posting collection-specific content must use the correct register: Black Rose = armor, defiance; Love Hurts = raw passion, the bloodline; Signature = standard, West Coast pride; Kids Capsule = little royalty, heritage. Cross-attribution is a brand violation.
- Full canon: `../skyyrose-content-engine/brand-guardrails.md`

## Artifact specification

The output of this skill is a complete, publication-ready **SkyyRose Social Media Policy** document. It must include all sections below, in order, at the specified detail level. The document is internal — not published publicly, but shared with every team member, community champion, and collaborator who represents the brand.

### Section 1: Purpose & Scope

**Length:** 2-3 paragraphs.
**Content:**
- Why this policy exists: to protect the brand, the community, and everyone who represents it
- Who it applies to: Corey Foster, anyone with access to official SkyyRose accounts, community champions, influencer partners, and contractors posting on behalf of SkyyRose
- What it covers: official brand accounts AND any content where someone explicitly references or represents SkyyRose
- Effective date and annual review commitment

**Voice:** Direct. This is an internal document, not marketing copy. Corey's voice — plain, specific, no preamble.

---

### Section 2: Brand Voice Standards

**Format:** Overview table + per-platform tone guidance.

**Voice Identity**

| We Are | We Are NOT |
|--------|-----------|
| Earned, direct, Oakland-specific | Hype-merchant, generic streetwear-brand noise |
| Confident in the craft | Defensive or promotional about quality |
| Community-rooted | Corporate-distant |
| Quietly premium (Fear of God energy, Oaklandish authenticity) | Loudly luxury (European fashion house posturing) |
| Specific about products and stories | Vague aspirational filler |

**Per-Platform Tone**

| Platform | Tone Adjustment |
|----------|----------------|
| **Instagram Feed** | Editorial, visual-first. Caption tells the product's story. 150-300 words. Hook before the fold. |
| **Instagram Reels/Stories** | Conversational, behind-the-scenes energy. Corey's natural voice when speaking. |
| **TikTok** | Most casual and raw. Founder energy. Real production footage, honest commentary. |
| **X / Twitter** | Concise, opinionated, Oakland-direct. Hot takes on craft and culture. Not customer service. |
| **Pinterest** | Silent — keyword-rich descriptions, no voice personality required. Product and lifestyle boards. |

**Topics we engage with:** Drop announcements, behind-the-scenes production, Oakland culture and landmarks, customer fit pics (with permission), brand story and founder perspective, fashion industry takes.

**Topics we avoid:** Politics (outside Oakland/Black culture advocacy, which is foundational to the brand), competitor attacks, internal drama, fake urgency, anything that reads as manufactured hype.

### Section 5: Community Champion & Collaborator Rules

**Format:** Rules list + what collaborators must never do.

Community champions (moderators in The Concrete Garden) and brand collaborators who post about SkyyRose are bound by the same voice standards as the brand team. In addition:

Champions MUST:
- Disclose their community champion role when discussing SkyyRose online: "I moderate The Concrete Garden community for SkyyRose"
- Post in the correct collection register if referencing a specific collection
- Refer to products by name (e.g., "BLACK Rose Crewneck"), not by SKU
- Escalate any public negative attention or controversy about the brand to Corey immediately

Champions MUST NOT:
- Post drop information or pre-order windows before the public launch date
- Represent SkyyRose positions on topics not explicitly covered by the brand (politics, controversies outside the brand's scope)
- Accept payment or gifts to post about SkyyRose without written disclosure to Corey first
- Use urgency manipulation language ("DROP NOW 🔥 LAST CHANCE") even in a personal capacity when it references SkyyRose products
- Cross-attribute collection voice (e.g., using Love Hurts language to promote a Black Rose product)

Influencer partners and paid collaborators are bound by a separate Brand Partnership Agreement that references this policy.

### Section 6: Crisis Communication Protocol

**Format:** Crisis levels table + step-by-step response protocol + holding statement template.

```
## What Qualifies as a Social Media Crisis

| Level | Trigger                                                          | Response Time |
|-------|------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|
| L1    | Single negative review or complaint with limited spread          | 24 hours      |
| L2    | Social thread gaining traction (50+ engagements); service issue  | 4-8 hours     |
| L3    | Viral negative attention; media coverage; shipping or product failure affecting 10+ customers | 1-2 hours |
| L4    | Brand-level reputation crisis (Corey statement required); cultural incident | Immediate |

## Crisis Response Steps

1. PAUSE — stop all scheduled posts across all platforms immediately
2. ASSESS — gather the facts before responding (who is affected, what happened,
   what is the current public exposure)
3. ESCALATE — notify Corey within 1 hour for any L2+ incident
4. DRAFT — prepare a response using the formula below
5. APPROVE — Corey approves every public response before it posts
6. RESPOND — post the approved response; do not respond in DMs to critics
   before the public statement is live
7. MONITOR — track the conversation every 2 hours for 48-72 hours
8. DEBRIEF — after resolution, document what happened, how we responded, and
   what changes to process or product are needed

## Response Formula (every crisis)
  1. Acknowledge what happened (no minimizing or corporate deflection)
  2. Express genuine empathy (not performative)
  3. State what we are doing about it
  4. Commit to a follow-up with a specific timeline

## Holding Statement Template
"We're aware of [issue]. We're looking into it now and will share a full update
within [timeframe]. If you're affected, reach out directly at [email/DM channel].
— SkyyRose"

Note: "soon" is not a timeline. Specify hours.
```

## Example: Policy Applied During a Signature Collection Drop

**Scenario:** A community champion posts on their personal Instagram: "The Signature Collection is officially dropping next Friday — pre-order opens at 8am PST 🔥 Don't miss it!!" — two days before the public announcement date.

**Policy violations:**
1. Drop information posted before public launch date (Section 5: Champions MUST NOT)
2. Urgency manipulation language ("Don't miss it!! 🔥") (Section 2: Voice Standards)
3. No Corey approval for a drop announcement (Section 4: Approval Workflow)

**Response:** Champion is notified immediately. Post is deleted (they own their personal account — they delete it; if they refuse, their champion role and community access are revoked). Formal warning issued per Section 8. Future posting on behalf of the brand requires 48-hour advance review. Signature Collection pre-order timing is assessed for whether it leaked to outside parties.

**Signature Collection voice check:** This collection's register is "stay golden," West Coast luxury, standard-setting. Even a properly timed announcement should carry that register — not "🔥 DON'T MISS IT 🔥."

## Anti-Patterns

- **Policy that only covers official accounts** — a community champion's personal post that leaks drop information is a brand crisis regardless of the account it came from. Scope must include all representatives.
- **"Use good judgment" as a voice guideline** — that phrase is meaningless without examples. The voice table and the anti-voice list in Section 2 replace "good judgment" with specific, enforceable standards.
- **No crisis plan until one is needed** — every brand encounters a social media crisis. A Love Hurts pre-order shipping delay hitting during peak season is a near-certainty. Plan the response before the crisis, not during it.
- **FTC compliance buried in a footnote** — paid partnership and gifting disclosures are legal requirements, not brand preferences. They warrant their own section and explicit team training.
- **Never distributing the policy** — a policy that team members haven't read and signed is theatre. The acknowledgment process is non-negotiable.
- **Hype language in the approved voice** — if the brand voice table doesn't explicitly exclude "🔥🔥 DON'T MISS OUT" energy, someone will use it. It must be named and prohibited.
- **No annual review date** — social media platforms change; brand voice evolves; FTC rules update. A policy without a review schedule becomes stale and misleading.
- **Using "soon" as a crisis response timeline** — "We'll share more information soon" is the single most trust-destroying phrase in a crisis. Every public commitment includes a specific time window.

---

## Recovery

- **An incident already happened before the policy existed:** Address the incident first (use `skyyrose-social-crisis-comms`), then write the policy immediately after resolution to prevent recurrence. Document the incident as the founding case study for training.
- **Team pushes back on restrictions:** Frame the policy as protection, not punishment. Every restriction exists because a real brand (likely not this one — not yet) paid a real cost. Show examples. Focus on empowering people to post confidently, not on preventing them from posting.
- **Policy is too long for busy collaborators:** Create the one-page quick reference (Section 9 output format). The full policy is what enforcement references; the summary is what people actually read.
- **No legal expertise in-house:** The compliance section is a starting point, not a legal opinion. For any regulated category (giveaways over $X, formal partnership deals), flag for legal review before executing. Note this explicitly in the policy document itself.
- **Solo brand (Corey only):** Write a personal social media code of conduct — same structure, self-directed. Use it as the foundation when collaborators join. Having the document in place before it's needed is the whole point.
