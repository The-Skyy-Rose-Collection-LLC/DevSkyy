# SkyyRose Social — Twitter / X Thread

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing a founder-perspective thread on the SkyyRose brand origin or Corey's story
- Launching a collection or product drop through a narrative thread (5-12 tweets)
- Publishing a "hot take" thread on fashion, streetwear, or Black-owned business
- Repurposing a brand story into a Twitter/X thread format for authority-building
- Building a "here's how we made this" thread for a garment or collection
- Writing a culture/community thread tied to Oakland, Bay Area, or Black fashion history

**DO NOT** use this for single tweets, Twitter ad copy, or DM templates. This is for threads of 5-15 tweets only. For short standalone posts, adapt the hook output from `skyyrose-social-thread-hook-writer`. For TikTok scripts, use `skyyrose-social-tiktok-script`.

---

## Brand Canon (non-negotiable)

- **Voice on X:** More direct and opinionated than other platforms. Oakland energy — unhurried, specific, no hedging. Corey speaks as a founder who has earned the room, not a brand trying to get it.
- **Tagline verbatim:** `Luxury Grows from Concrete.` — appears in the CTA tweet only (once, period included).
- **Collection register locked:** Black Rose = armor, defiance. Love Hurts = bloodline, raw romance. Signature = "stay golden," West Coast standard. Never cross-attribute. A thread about one collection stays in that register throughout.
- **No urgency manipulation:** Drop announcements state scarcity as fact ("250 made, pre-order is open"). No countdown pressure, no "last chance" desperation tone.
- **No European luxury-house references.** The Five only: Kith, Oaklandish, Culture Kings, Fear of God, Palm Angels.
- **Links only in the final tweet.** Mid-thread links kill engagement and Twitter suppresses them.

Full canon: `../skyyrose-content-engine/brand-guardrails.md`

## Phase 2: Outline

### Thread Architecture

Every SkyyRose thread follows this spine:

1. **Hook tweet** — stops the scroll; earns the "show this thread" click
2. **Context tweet** — why this matters, who Corey is, or what the collection stands for
3. **Body tweets (3-N-2)** — the substance: story beats, product details, cultural anchors, founder lessons
4. **Summary tweet** — designed to be screenshotted; the TL;DR that earns the share
5. **CTA tweet** — single action + `Luxury Grows from Concrete.` + link

### Outline Format

```
Hook:     [First tweet — must earn "show this thread" click]
Context:  [Why this matters / stakes / who is speaking]
Body:
  Tweet 3: [Story beat or point 1]
  Tweet 4: [Story beat or point 2]
  Tweet 5: [Story beat or point 3 — include one concrete detail or fact]
  Tweet 6: [Story beat or point 4]
Summary:  [TL;DR — shareable on its own as a screenshot]
CTA:      [Follow + link + tagline]
```

**GATE: Approve outline before writing full tweets. Every body tweet must stand alone — no tweet should require reading the previous one to make sense.**

## Phase 3: Write

### Tweet-by-Tweet Rules

**Hook Tweet (Tweet 1)**
- Under 280 characters — no exceptions.
- SkyyRose hook formats (pick one):
  - Founder result: "I built a luxury streetwear brand out of Oakland with no investors. Here's what nobody tells you about that:"
  - Identity statement: "Black Rose isn't a product. It's an answer. A thread:"
  - Contrarian take: "Luxury doesn't come from Europe. It never did. Thread:"
  - Specific detail: "The embroidery on the BLACK Rose Crewneck took four rounds to get right. Here's why we didn't cut it:"
  - Culture call: "Oakland doesn't get enough credit for what it's building. Let me fix that:"
- End with a thread indicator: "A thread:" or "(thread)" or "Here's what I mean:"

**Context Tweet (Tweet 2)**
- Credibility and stakes — who is speaking, why this collection exists, what this thread is actually about.
- No filler. "First, some context:" is fine if it leads directly to substance.
- Under 280 characters.

**Body Tweets (Tweets 3 to N-2)**
- One idea per tweet. Never two.
- Start each with a number ("3/") or a strong opening word — not "And..." or "Also..."
- Line breaks between ideas within a tweet for readability on mobile.
- Vary format: some tweets are declarative, some are mini-stories, one should contain a specific fact or detail.
- At least one tweet in a product thread must name the garment by name (not SKU) with one concrete construction detail.

**Summary Tweet (Second-to-Last)**
- "TL;DR:" or "To recap:" — then the 3 key points, bulleted if space allows.
- Designed to be screenshotted and shared. Should work without reading the thread.
- Keep under 240 characters if possible so it screenshotshots cleanly.

**CTA Tweet (Final)**
- Single action only.
- If drop: link to the product page (first and only link in the thread).
- Always close with `Luxury Grows from Concrete.`
- Add 1-2 hashtags maximum here: `#SkyyRose` + one collection tag.

### Formatting Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Character limit** | Every tweet under 280 characters — no exceptions |
| **Numbering** | Body tweets: "3/" at the start |
| **Line breaks** | Blank lines between ideas in longer tweets |
| **Emojis** | 0-1 per tweet max. Use as a visual marker, not decoration. |
| **Hashtags** | Zero in the thread body. 1-2 in final CTA tweet only. |
| **Links** | Final tweet ONLY. Never mid-thread. |

## Phase 4: Polish

### Thread Quality Checklist

```
- [ ] Hook tweet earns "show this thread" — passes the "would I click?" test
- [ ] Every tweet under 280 characters
- [ ] Each body tweet delivers one clear idea, numbered consistently
- [ ] At least one body tweet contains a specific fact, detail, or mini-story
- [ ] Summary tweet works as a standalone screenshot
- [ ] CTA tweet has single action + "Luxury Grows from Concrete." + max 2 hashtags
- [ ] No links mid-thread (final tweet only)
- [ ] Collection voice verified — no cross-attribution
- [ ] No urgency manipulation language
- [ ] No European luxury house name-drops
- [ ] Thread reads as a continuous narrative, but each tweet also stands alone
```

### Pinned Tweet Option

For brand-story threads with long shelf life, provide an alternate version of the hook tweet optimized to work as a profile pin — slightly more evergreen framing than a time-sensitive drop hook.

### Posting Notes

- Best times for SkyyRose on X: 8-10 AM or 5-7 PM PST
- Post tweet 1, then the rest 1-2 minutes apart as replies to tweet 1
- Quote-tweet the hook 6-8 hours later with one key insight to resurface it
- Don't delete and repost to game timing — reply engagement is more valuable than repost reach

## Anti-Patterns

- **Hook tweet is a statement, not a hook** — "SkyyRose just dropped a new collection" is a press release, not a thread hook. Every hook creates a reason to click "show thread."
- **Links mid-thread** — Twitter's algorithm suppresses mid-thread links. One link, final tweet only.
- **Wall-of-text tweets** — 280 characters of unbroken text on a phone screen is unreadable. Use line breaks.
- **Cross-attributing collection language** — a Signature thread must not contain "bloodline that raised me" (Love Hurts) or "armor" (Black Rose). Collection register is locked.
- **Monotone format** — every body tweet as "N/ [statement]" gets pattern-blind by tweet 5. Mix in a mini-story, a specific construction detail, a question.
- **Hashtag stuffing in the body** — #BlackOwnedFashion mid-thread feels like an ad insert. Hashtags belong in the CTA tweet only.
- **European luxury reference** — a thread that name-drops Bottega or references "old-money European" aesthetics is the wrong brand DNA. The Five only.
- **Urgency pressure language** — "Pre-order NOW before it closes" is not Corey's register. "Pre-order is open" stated once in the CTA is enough.
- **Thread over 12 tweets** — attention drops after tweet 10 for SkyyRose's audience. Cut or split.
- **Summary tweet that's just the hook again** — the summary must deliver new synthesis, not repeat tweet 1.

---

## Recovery

- **No hook ideas:** Generate 5 variants using different formulas (identity statement, contrarian take, specific-detail, founder-result, culture-call). Present all 5 — let Corey or the team pick.
- **Thread too long:** Cut any body tweet that overlaps with another. If two tweets make the same point from different angles, keep the one with a concrete detail.
- **Voice feels too polished / corporate:** Read it aloud with Oakland cadence. If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, rewrite. Contractions, short sentences, specific proper nouns (The Town, Bay, Oakland) are the register markers.
- **CTA tweet underperforming (low click-through):** Make the link the only item in the tweet after the tagline. Competing asks dilute click intent.
- **Collection register drift:** If you're unsure whether a phrase belongs to the right collection, read `docs/brand/collection-stories.md` before finalizing. The per-collection registers are there verbatim.
