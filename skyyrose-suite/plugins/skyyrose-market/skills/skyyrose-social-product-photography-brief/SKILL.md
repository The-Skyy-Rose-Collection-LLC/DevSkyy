# SkyyRose Social — Product Photography Brief

## When to Use This Skill

- Briefing a photographer for WooCommerce product detail page (PDP) images
- Planning garment-specific shots for a new drop (flat lay, on-body, detail macros)
- Preparing product images for IG Feed, Pinterest, and social ad use
- Defining background, lighting, and styling direction per collection for a tabletop or studio session
- Producing catalog-compliant imagery that feeds `skyyrose/elite_studio` and the WooCommerce media library
- Building a repeatable shot template across multiple products in the same collection

**DO NOT** use this for lifestyle/editorial/campaign brand shoots — use `skyyrose-social-brand-photography-brief` for sessions featuring talent, Oakland locations, and brand storytelling. This skill is for garment-centric product photography: the shots that live on the PDP and convert the buyer.

---

## Brand Canon (non-negotiable)

- **The Five visual references — pull from these exclusively:** Kith (editorial product storytelling, elevated retail polish), Oaklandish (authentic town pride visible even in product styling), Culture Kings (bold streetwear merchandising, hype-energy presentation), Fear of God (quiet luxury — let the garment speak through restraint), Palm Angels (street-luxe graphic confidence, garment as statement). Never Bottega Veneta, Hedi Slimane, Rick Owens, 032c, Acne, Givenchy-by-Tisci, or any European luxury-house aesthetic in product photography. Wrong brand DNA.
- **Lockup images, never live type:** The collection name must never appear as typed text in any product photo. If a hero crop will receive a collection-name overlay, leave clean negative space at the top of frame — the lockup asset from `assets/images/hero-overlays/` (BR/LH/SIG) or `assets/images/logos/` (Kids) is composited at the Elite Studio stage.
- **Per-collection background and lighting:** Black Rose = dark/concrete, cool-dramatic. Love Hurts = deep red-brick or dark surfaces, crimson-warm. Signature = warm wood, natural stone, golden tone. Kids Capsule = clean, bright, warm-neutral.
- **Color tokens:** Rose Gold `#B76E79`, Dark `#0A0A0A`, Silver `#C0C0C0`, Crimson `#DC143C`, Gold `#D4AF37`.
- **Products referenced by NAME, not SKU.** "Black Rose Crewneck" in the brief — not "br-001." Photographers don't know the internal catalog.
- **Product facts from catalog only.** Never invent colorways, materials, or features. Resolve against `wordpress-theme/skyyrose-flagship/data/skyyrose-catalog.csv` before briefing.
- Full canon: `../skyyrose-content-engine/brand-guardrails.md`

## Phase 2: Per-Collection Background and Lighting Direction

Apply the correct collection direction to every shot in the brief. Do not blend or improvise.

### Black Rose — Dark Precision

- **Hero background:** Dark concrete (#0A0A0A), dark slate, or matte black seamless
- **Lifestyle background:** Concrete wall, raw industrial surface, dark wood
- **Lighting (hero/flat lay):** Dual softbox at 45 degrees, slight edge light from above to separate dark garment from dark ground; fill card below for shadow detail
- **Lighting (detail):** Single key from left at 30 degrees, raking across texture; no fill — let shadow define the weave or print
- **Lighting (lifestyle/on-body):** Low-key; one softbox camera-left, no rim light unless needed to define silhouette
- **Styling notes:** Silver hardware and accessories only. No gold. No warm props.
- **AVOID:** White backgrounds, warm color grading, soft-light evenly-lit look

### Love Hurts — Crimson Heat

- **Hero background:** Deep red brick (if prop available), dark maroon seamless, or black
- **Lifestyle background:** Cracked sidewalk, red-brick alley wall, dark wood with crimson accent prop
- **Lighting (hero/flat lay):** Dual softbox; acceptable to use a subtle red-gel fill on one side to add warmth to shadow areas on dark garments
- **Lighting (detail):** Directional from left, warm color temp — let crimson elements glow slightly
- **Lighting (lifestyle):** Warm dramatic — dusk light or warm strobe with slight red cast
- **Styling notes:** Crimson accessories, red-toned props acceptable (not Valentine's florals — think raw, urban red)
- **AVOID:** Pink or pastel props, Valentine's Day clichés, soft-focus romance aesthetics

### Signature — West Coast Gold

- **Hero background:** Warm cream, natural stone, warm white seamless, light-toned wood
- **Lifestyle background:** Warm wood desk, cream linen, golden-hour outdoor
- **Lighting (hero/flat lay):** Natural window light or softbox with warm gel; even and bright; slight shadow on right for dimension
- **Lighting (detail):** Warm directional — morning window light from left reveals texture without harshness
- **Lighting (lifestyle):** Natural golden hour outdoor, or warm studio approximation
- **Styling notes:** Gold jewelry, warm leather goods as props. Neutral wood and linen surfaces.
- **AVOID:** Cool or blue-toned lighting, industrial or dark backgrounds, anything that reads Black Rose

### Kids Capsule — Bright Royalty

- **Hero background:** White seamless, warm white, or soft cream
- **Lifestyle background:** Clean park setting, Oakland mural wall (if color-consistent), warm-neutral studio
- **Lighting (hero/flat lay):** Bright and even — dual softbox, white reflectors, no dramatic shadow; kids' pieces need clarity
- **Lighting (detail):** Soft directional, minimal shadow, high brightness
- **Lifestyle:** Natural outdoor light, morning or overcast — never direct harsh sun
- **Styling notes:** Rose gold and neutral accessories. Clean, bright surfaces. Props: minimal — a ball, a book, a simple toy (nothing branded outside SkyyRose)
- **AVOID:** Dark backgrounds, dramatic/moody lighting, adult editorial aesthetics

## Phase 5: Format the Brief

Compile into a single photographer-ready document with these sections:
1. Product overview (name, collection, variant count, key selling points)
2. Session scope (shot list type: 7-shot catalog or extended)
3. Visual direction (background, lighting, styling — per-collection from Phase 2)
4. Shot list (table with all shots: description, framing, lighting, background)
5. Platform image requirements (dimension table, file specs)
6. Styling notes (props, surfaces, human elements, AVOID list)
7. File naming convention
8. Elite Studio pipeline notes

## Visual Direction — Black Rose

**Background (hero/flat lay):** Matte black seamless or dark concrete surface
**Background (detail):** Neutral dark surface — let the garment fill the frame
**Lighting (hero/flat lay):** Dual softbox at 45 degrees each side; edge light from above or behind to separate black crewneck from black ground; white fill card below to add shadow detail without hot spots
**Lighting (detail macros):** Single directional key at 30 degrees from left — raking across embroidery or fabric weave. No fill. Shadow is texture.
**Color temperature:** Cool-neutral throughout. No warm grade.
**Styling:** No props. The crewneck is the protagonist. Dark slate or concrete surface only. No linen, no wood, no warm textures.
**AVOID:** White backgrounds (not this collection — use for Kids or Signature only), warm-toned surfaces, any prop that reads "lifestyle" or soft

## Styling Notes

**Props:** None for this session. Black Rose product photography uses the garment as the sole subject.
**Surfaces:** Matte black seamless (hero shots). Dark concrete or slate (flat lay and detail).
**Human elements:** None for this session. If hands are added in a follow-up session: matte dark fabric sleeve or bare hand — no warm-toned knit.
**AVOID:**
- Shiny or reflective surfaces that create hot spots on dark matte fabric
- Warm or golden color grading — this is Black Rose, not Signature
- Props that soften the aesthetic (candles, foliage, coffee mugs)
- Overhead flash that flattens the embroidery texture — raking light is required for detail shots
- Stock-photo flat-lay arrangements with scattered accessories

`{collection-slug}-{product-slug}-{shot-type}-{sequence}-{crop}.jpg`

Examples:

- `black-rose-crewneck-detail-embroidery-04-square.jpg`

## Deliverables

- RAW + edited JPEG (minimum 2048px on shortest side for all catalog shots)
- Color grading: cool-neutral, consistent across all Black Rose shots in this session. No warm shift.
- Retouching: remove lint, sensor dust, and fiber artifacts. No background cleanup beyond what's already clean. Preserve fabric texture — no heavy smoothing.
- Crops per shot: square (1:1), 4:5, 2:3 from all flat lay and hero shots
- Delivery: Drive folder organized as `black-rose / crewneck / hero`, `black-rose / crewneck / detail`, `black-rose / crewneck / flat-lay`
- Timeline: confirm with photographer at brief handoff

## Anti-Patterns

- **Never use a white background for Black Rose or Love Hurts product shots.** White background = Kids Capsule or Signature (light variants only). Applying white to Black Rose destroys the collection's visual identity in the catalog.
- **Never type-render the collection name in the product photo.** The lockup image from `assets/images/hero-overlays/` is composited by Elite Studio — burning it into photography makes it uncorrectable.
- **Never pull European luxury-house product photography aesthetics.** No Bottega-style hand-draped fabric in soft Venetian light, no Rick Owens negative space minimalism, no Acne Studio sterile Scandinavian white. Pull from the Five: Kith, Oaklandish, Culture Kings, Fear of God, Palm Angels.
- **Never reference the product by SKU in the brief.** "Black Rose Crewneck" — not "br-001." The brief goes to photographers; SKU-first causes set confusion and has caused product conflations before.
- **Never invent product details.** If a selling point or feature isn't confirmed in the catalog CSV or a dossier, don't include it in the brief. Don't describe embroidery that doesn't exist.
- **Never apply warm color grading to Black Rose or Love Hurts shots.** Collection identity lives in the color tone. A warm grade accidentally applied to Black Rose makes it read Signature.
- **Never skip the scale/fit shot** (on-body) for apparel, even if this session is flat-lay focused. Schedule it for a follow-up if model isn't available — but note the gap in the brief so the PDP isn't launched with only flat lay.
- **Never submit a brief without an AVOID list.** Photographers unfamiliar with SkyyRose will default to their own aesthetic vocabulary. The AVOID list is what prevents a Fashion Nova or Zara product-photo aesthetic from entering the catalog.
- **Never cross background/lighting direction between collections.** If running a mixed-collection session, shoot all Black Rose shots consecutively before changing the set to Signature. Never mix backgrounds on the same shoot day without a deliberate set change.
- **Never deliver product images with visible pricing tags or non-SkyyRose branding.** Check for hangtags, external labels, and other-brand props in every frame before delivery.

---

## Recovery

- **Photographer defaults to white background for all shots:** Catch this at the brief review, not on shoot day. Include the collection background direction prominently in the brief header, not buried in a table. Send a reference image for the exact background type before the session.
- **Dark fabric disappears into dark background (Black Rose):** Add edge lighting — a strobe or LED strip from directly above or slightly behind the garment to create a thin separation rim. Adjust, don't switch backgrounds.
- **Macro detail shots look flat (no texture):** The key light angle is wrong. Move it lower and more to the side — raking light must be at 20-30 degrees, not 45 or above. If natural light, move the product to catch a sharper angle from the window.
- **Garment has multiple colorways and time is short:** Shoot the hero front for all colorways first (same setup, swap the garment, don't move lights). Then add angle/back/detail shots for the hero colorway in depth. Catalog requires a hero for each variant — everything else is additive.
- **Brief handed to a photographer who has never shot streetwear:** Add five reference images from the Five (one from each brand) to the physical brief packet. Visual references override a paragraph of description every time.
- **Delivered shots have wrong color grading:** Return to photographer with collection color direction from this brief before requesting a reshoot. Most grading errors are fixable in post — full reshoot is last resort.
