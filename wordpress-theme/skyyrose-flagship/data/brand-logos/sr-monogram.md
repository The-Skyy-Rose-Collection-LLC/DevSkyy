# SR Monogram (without rose) — Canonical Reference

The SkyyRose lettermark used on Black Rose collection products as a secondary
brand mark. Distinct from the canonical Black Rose logo (which is the
three-rose-cluster art) — this monogram is the brand's typographic mark.

## Visual Description

A tightly interlocked **S** + **R** monogram in a stylized dimensional serif
script:

- **The "S"** sits on the left, drawn as a thick swooping serif with classic
  ball-terminal swashes at the top and bottom curves of the letter.
- **The "R"** sits on the right, slightly larger and overlapping the S — its
  bowl interlocks with the upper curve of the S, and its leg sweeps out to
  the lower-right with a flourishing serif tail.
- **A small "i" dot** (a single tittle) is positioned inside the bowl of
  the R — a subtle stylistic flourish.
- The letterforms are rendered with **dimensional 3D shading** — gradient
  highlights and shadows give the impression of beveled / polished gold or
  copper material. Lighter highlights along the upper edges, deeper warm
  shadows along the lower edges and inside curves.
- **Color palette**: warm metallic gold-to-copper gradient — primarily
  tones of copper, antique gold, and bronze. NOT flat gold. NOT yellow.
  NOT silver. NOT chrome.
- The composition is **roughly square / slightly horizontal** — the
  interlocked S+R sits in a compact balanced cluster.

## What this monogram IS NOT

- NOT a Black Rose logo (the rose-cluster) — it does NOT contain any rose,
  vine, cloud, or plant element.
- NOT a flat single-color mark — the gold dimensional shading is integral
  to its identity.
- NOT a sans-serif treatment.
- NOT the SkyyRose Collection script wordmark (that's a different mark used
  in the Authentic Collection patch lower section).

## How techniques apply this monogram

- **Embroidered** (e.g., on the Black Rose Baseball Classic jersey at
  back-neck): rendered in gold-tone thread, with subtle shading approximated
  via varied stitch direction or two-tone thread. Typically ~2in wide.
- **Heat-transfer / printed**: full-color rendering preserves the gradient.
- **Embossed / debossed**: rendered as a tonal raised relief in the base
  fabric color (like an embossed leather stamp).
