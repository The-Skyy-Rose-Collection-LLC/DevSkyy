# Love Hurts — Designer Copy

> Source: docs/brand/collection-stories.md (canonical). Seed: "Beauty and the Beast told from the Beast's perspective, through luxury attire."

## Origin

"Hurts" is not a theme. It is the bloodline that raised the founder.

Corey's own framing (locked 2026-05-23):

> "Hurts is the bloodline that raised me."

That is the line. Not maternal grandmother's surname, not extended family side — the people who raised him. The grandmother in `collection-content.php` is one voice from that bloodline; the "three generations of Hurts" on the landing page are the same people across time. Foster is the surname Corey carries publicly; Hurts is the line that shaped who carries it. Both are true. This collection is for the second one.

The narrative centers on a family that loved without stopping despite the world pouring pain back in return. The Beast & the rose motif runs through the collection — the Beast didn't hide from ugliness, he hid because he loved something fragile.

*Sources: `inc/collection-content.php`, `template-landing-love-hurts.php`, `skyyrose.co/about/`*

## Voice & Mood

Heavy but not defeated. The voice of someone who watched their family survive — not just endure, survive — and chose to name a collection after the survival instead of the wound.

Hero tagline (verbatim, live site): **"They called me Beast. They were right."**

Hero subtitle, verbatim: *"But even the Beast kept a rose under glass — protecting the most fragile thing he ever loved. This collection carries the weight of three generations of Hurts."*

Landing page story title, verbatim: *"This Isn't a Theme. It's a Family Name."*

Grandmother's voice, verbatim from `collection-content.php`:

> "My grandmother used to say it plain: 'Love hurts, baby. That's how you know it's real.' She wasn't being poetic. She was teaching survival."
